From Modest Dreams to Sustainable Triumphs: The Tumaliktik Farmers' Journey Towards Agricultural Resilience​

Lovely Joy L. Saludez | DA-Caraga | Viewers: ​

"At first, our association's dreams were modest, just enough to sustain us as rice farmers day by day. Yet, we soon realized that to fulfill our aspirations for our families, we needed to aim higher," shared Merlina Plaza, Chairperson of the Tumaliktik Farmers Association. "The desire to secure a better future through farming led us to redouble our efforts."

Merlina Plaza, Chairperson of the Tumaliktik Farmers Association, reflects on their journey from humble beginnings to ambitious goals in Buena Gracia, Talacogon, Agusan del Sur. Situated in an area prone to climate-related challenges due to its low elevation, the association faced adversity from floods, strong winds, and typhoons. Despite these obstacles, their determination to succeed drove them to seek support from the Department of Agriculture's Adaptation and Mitigation Initiatives in Agriculture (DA-AMIA) project.​

In 2021, their efforts were celebrated as they achieved recognition as the third Amia Village in Agusan del Sur province. This marked a turning point, bringing essential agricultural inputs and machinery to bolster their rice, vegetable, duck, chicken, and tilapia production. Through interventions like the egg machine, floating tiller, and pump and engine set, provided by DA-AMIA, the association not only enhanced their farming capabilities but also fortified their resilience against environmental challenges.​

To develop food sustainability and earn more, the association was taught to practice a diversified farming system for its 2-hectare farm. They practice crop rotation for vegetables, and intercropping for upland areas, strategies that not only enhance productivity but also contribute to sustainable farming practices.​

​"At first, we thought of focusing on one commodity such as rice, but through AMIA, we were taught to plant multiple crop varieties in a single field, integrating livestock and aquaculture so we can thrive despite the challenges posed by climate change," Merlina said.

Not too long after they became an association in February 2023, the Philippine Center for Postharvest Development and Mechanization (PhilMech) provided an additional one unit each of floating tiller, Pump and Engine Set, Combine Harvester, Hauling Truck, and other agricultural inputs, boosting the production of the members.​

According to Merlina, rice production has seen a remarkable improvement, rising from four tons per hectare to seven tons. The egg production rate from chickens and ducks now stands impressively at 90% daily. Additionally, the association harvests 300 kilograms of tilapia every six months, showcasing their diversified and thriving agricultural output.​

​The increased productivity has translated into tangible benefits for the Tumaliktik Farmers Association. With the profits generated, they have successfully constructed essential infrastructure including a machinery shed, a comfort room, and storage facilities for feed. They have also installed electrical and water systems, enhancing their operational efficiency and sustainability.

The adoption of AMIA-recommended technologies has been pivotal for the association's success. Merlina Plaza views this as a significant milestone achieved after three years of steadfast support from AMIA.​

"This success is a testament to how our lives have transformed. We now enjoy a steady income, ensuring that we can savor every meal with our staple rice paired with a sumptuous viand—a simple pleasure once beyond our reach, Moreover, we can provide our families with a comfortable home and send our children to school. AMIA has truly been our shield, our guiding light-our everything," Plaza expressed proudly.​

Just recently, the agency also allocated an extra P100,000 for Balut and Salted Egg Enterprise which is expected to ensure the sustainability of beneficiaries' income. In heartfelt appreciation for the assistance they received, the Tumaliktik Farmers Association is now paying it forward by supporting other aspiring farmers. They have initiated a program to help newcomers interested in starting a duck enterprise, generously providing them with free ducks to kick-start their own ventures.​

Merlina, alongside her dedicated 46 members, acknowledges the journey they have undertaken and the challenges that still lie ahead. However, their resolve remains steadfast, united in their commitment to overcoming obstacles and securing a sustainable future. They attribute much of their progress to the valuable training and workshops provided by the Department of Agriculture, which have significantly enhanced their skills in climate-resilient agriculture and sustainable farm management.​

​These enhancements emphasize the association's spirit of gratitude and community support, as well as their ongoing dedication to continuous improvement and resilience in agriculture.
